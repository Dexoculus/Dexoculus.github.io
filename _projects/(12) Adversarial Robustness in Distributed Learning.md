## Overview

Ongoing joint capstone project with the Information and Intelligent Security Lab at the University of Nevada, Las Vegas, running from September 2025 to June 2026.

## Contributions

- Architected a reproducible federated learning pipeline with Flower to simulate distributed training and fine-tune YOLOv8 on traffic-signal datasets.
- Built Raspberry Pi-based edge vehicles and deployed trained object-detection models for real-time physical inference.
- Designed physical security validation experiments by injecting adversarial perturbations into deployed models.
- Evaluated how attacks propagate from model-level weaknesses into autonomous edge behavior.

## Status

The project is ongoing. Current work focuses on repeatable attack and defense evaluation across distributed training, model deployment, and physical edge execution.
