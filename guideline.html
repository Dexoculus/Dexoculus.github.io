PRINCIPLES

Integrated Design Direction Without Image References

This document is not a screenshot gallery. It is an implementation guide for reproducing the intended interface mood with native web UI: contour backgrounds, restrained halo geometry, white information surfaces, black operation panels, yellow selection states, and limited semantic colors.

Vector Contour Field

Use irregular inline SVG paths to create topographic terrain lines from a straight top-down view. Keep them sparse, pale, and structural so the terrain mood stays flat and readable.

Halo Geometry

Halo elements sit between decoration and navigation. They can mark focus, routing, system identity, or module grouping. Use thin blue/yellow orbits and compact focus circles instead of large background ellipses or busy spoke grids.

White Field / Black Control

White surfaces carry reading, analysis, and catalog content. Black panels carry operation, confirmation, and high-density decisions. Yellow should identify the final action or current selection.

Sparse Grid / Dot Matrix

For CV and portfolio pages, prefer side-placed vertical grid sectors and subtle dot matrices in unused corners. Keep the central reading field clear and never scatter short decorative lines across content.

COLOR POLICY

Black And White As Base, Yellow As Focus

Blue has a visible role in halo lines and progress traces, but it is not the main brand color. The center of the system remains black, white, gray, and yellow. Red, orange, blue, cyan, purple, pink, and green are reserved for state, energy, rarity, or special signals.

Palette Operating Rule Black and white define the structural surface. Gray creates hierarchy, inactive states, dividers, and soft depth. Yellow and gold are reserved for active tabs, current focus, CTA, reward, and primary progress. Red, orange, blue, cyan, purple, pink, and green must not become the dominant atmosphere.
Integration Rule Endfield contributes operational density, black panels, yellow selection, and strong status colors. Decagrammaton contributes clean white fields, sacred circular rhythm, pale blue halo logic, and laboratory calm. Use both inside one black-white-yellow system instead of splitting the UI into separate modes.

SHAPE SYSTEM

Use Halo Elements More Actively, But Keep Them Thin

Circular elements should be more present than before, but they must behave like UI structure: rings, target radius, navigation orbit, progress path, selection field, or system diagram. Avoid heavy decorative circles and dense radial grids.

Halo Ring

Use thin elliptical marks that combine pale blue order with yellow focus. Rings should guide attention, not fill the screen.

Pill Control

Use pill controls for primary CTAs, filters, and compact selection. Keep text short and center-aligned.

Contour Map

Generate terrain mood with thin vector contour paths from a vertical map view. Avoid rotated, perspective-skewed, or perfectly concentric contour fields.

State Route

Use red, orange, blue, and cyan only where the UI needs a semantic route, alert, progress marker, or power state.

IMPLEMENTATION SPEC

Agent-Ready Design Guidelines

The final direction is a standalone code-native brand system. Increase the presence of white space and quiet node rhythm, preserve yellow-centered decision logic, and restrict secondary colors to state-specific moments.

Asset Policy
Do not attach external images, screenshots, map files, fonts, SVG packs, or CDN assets. Build all background fields, map mood, rings, simple icons, and diagram elements with native HTML, CSS, and inline SVG only.
Background System
For the current CV/portfolio implementation, use sparse side-placed vertical grid sectors with small dot-matrix fields instead of hand-placed contour paths. Leave the central reading field clear, keep grid spacing broad, normally around 96px to 120px, and keep line opacity low so the background creates tactical depth without reducing text legibility. Contour motifs may remain as secondary brand vocabulary only when they do not read as decorative noise.
Layout Ratio
Favor a clean white central field, black operational modules, a dark tactical header, and compact side panels. The composition should feel technical and readable rather than decorative or poster-like.
Modern Simplicity
Prefer restraint over spectacle. Keep contour maps, hatch patterns, halo glow, dot matrices, and yellow highlight bands below the threshold where they compete with content. Place hatching only on corners or short edge zones instead of covering whole surfaces, and keep central navigation and reading areas free of hatch texture. Use fewer shadows, flatter cards, quieter borders, and larger calm surfaces so the interface feels current rather than overloaded.
Section Divider
On white or pale information cards, separate title and body with a 2px solid Decagrammaton Core Gold line, #febb0c. Do not use weak gray separators for card headers. Dark operational panels may keep a brighter yellow glow line when the module is active.
Color Priority
Use black and white as primary colors, gray as hierarchy, and yellow as focus. Red, orange, blue, cyan, green, purple, and pink are semantic or rarity colors and should appear in small controlled areas.
Data Colorbars
Use four colorbar families. Default scalar data uses neutral-to-core: #fefefe -> #e1e2e4 -> #909090 -> #505050 -> #2b2b2b -> #febb0c -> #ffef01. Signed delta data uses system-to-focus: #1288f8 -> #7cd0ff -> #fefefe -> #fbef73 -> #febb0c. Risk overlays use semantic alert only: #e1e2e4 -> #808080 -> #ff7000 -> #e42525. Halo signal data uses special-system colors: #1288f8 -> #7cd0ff -> #00d6fa -> #efbbe5 -> #ff99c6 -> #f456a4. Avoid rainbow maps.
Dark Mode
Do not simply invert the light theme. Treat dark mode as a night-operation variant: keep yellow as the active decision signal, convert white information fields into charcoal glass surfaces, preserve thin blue/yellow halo rings, and reduce contour opacity so the interface feels calm rather than noisy.
Theme Control
Expose a compact header toggle that switches between light and dark modes without reloading. Persist the user choice in local storage, but allow the URL parameters ?theme=light and ?theme=dark to override it for previews and QA.
Halo Geometry
Use compact halo orbits, circular focus chips, thin borders, and soft blue/yellow traces. Orbit labels such as MAP, LOG, DATA, AI, and RUN should sit inside round nodes, not pill tags. Avoid oversized concentric ellipses, dense spoke patterns, heavy mandalas, or rings that run beneath large text. Compact radar widgets may keep a small dashed ellipse when it improves module readability.
Corner Language
Panels should use small rounded corners, usually 4px to 8px. Primary CTAs and filters may use pill shapes. Avoid overly sharp polygon cuts and avoid large rounded marketing cards.
Header Behavior
The top navigation uses a dark tactical bar. The selected tab turns yellow and visually returns every other tab to gray or black. Keep hatch texture limited to the far left and far right side zones; do not run hatching underneath the central navigation labels or over the brand lockup. Do not add console pad labels such as L1, R1, RB, or L3.
Typography
Use Pretendard for UI labels, headings, cards, and readable prose. Use D2Coding for code-like readouts, telemetry, hex values, section kickers, and compact system metadata.
Component Rules
White cards represent information and browsing. Black panels represent operation and confirmation. Yellow marks selected tabs, focus modules, action buttons, and primary progress. Semantic colors must always include a functional reason. Dot matrices may appear in unused card corners, especially near action controls or empty terminal-like regions, but content must always sit above the dot layer and remain readable.
Interaction Standard
Use CSS for primary hover and focus states: raise circular chips by 1px to 2px, strengthen the border to yellow or Core Gold, and add a restrained glow. Use JavaScript only as progressive enhancement that updates CSS variables, such as --orbit-x and --orbit-y, for pointer-reactive halo motion. Do not mutate layout, resize cards, or move text during hover. Respect prefers-reduced-motion and reset pointer effects on leave or cancel.
Accessibility
Do not place halo labels or panel overlays on top of large headings. Maintain high contrast for body text, keep animated or patterned areas subtle, and ensure long English labels wrap cleanly on mobile.
